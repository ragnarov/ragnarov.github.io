How to read :
Read from left to right until decimal multiplier band.
Then multiply this number with the decimal multiplier.

Identifying the first coloured band :
  • Tolerance bands such as gold and silver have special colours.
    These are usually the last band and belong to the right side of the resistor.
  • To distinguish left from right there is usually a gap before tolerance band.
    This separates the bands into two groups.
    Put larger group to the left.
  • Sometimes the first band is closest to the end.
Values of different colours according to position.

Trivial Stuff :
  • All coded components have at least two value bands and a multiplier. Other bands are optional.
  • Resistors with tolerance less than 2% are called "Precision Resistors".
  • Zero ohm resistors are marked with a single black band.
  • Precision resistors may be marked with a five band system,
    First three bands indicate significant digits, then a power of 10 multiplier, and a tolerance band.
Body Colour : These are not the same as band colours.
Sometimes these colours are manufacturer specific.
Blue coloured resistors usually mean non-flammable.
These will not burn if they overheat.
White resistors usually mean fusible.

Caution : If these types of resistors are found in circuits then do not replace these with normal resistors.
